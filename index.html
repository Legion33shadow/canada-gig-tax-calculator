Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I pay double CPP as a gig worker?

Employed Canadians split CPP 50/50 with their employer. As a self-employed worker you pay both halves — currently 11.9% of net income above $3,500, up to YMPE of $73,200. On $60,000 net income: ~$7,100 CPP vs ~$3,550 for a salaried employee at the same gross. This is the single biggest hidden cost of gig work in Canada.

What is the GST/HST $30,000 threshold?

Once your total taxable supplies exceed $30,000 in any quarter or over four consecutive quarters, you must register within 29 days. Failing to register means CRA assesses the GST on your past revenue — you absorb the cost — plus a 6% penalty and compounded daily interest. After registering you can claim Input Tax Credits on all business purchases, partially offsetting the cost.

Can I deduct home office costs?

Yes, for self-employed individuals if the space is used exclusively and regularly for work. Calculate the percentage by square footage. Deductible: proportional rent or mortgage interest, utilities, internet, property taxes. Keep monthly receipts — CRA digital audits now match declared deductions against bank transaction patterns.

Do I need to pay quarterly tax instalments?

If your net tax owing exceeds $3,000 ($1,800 in Quebec) for both the current year and either of the two prior years, CRA requires instalments: March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15. Missing these triggers 9% annualized prescribed-rate interest in 2026. Set up a recurring calendar reminder and auto-transfer on those dates.

Is incorporating worth it?

Generally yes if net income consistently exceeds $80,000–$100,000. A CCPC pays 9% federal small business rate on first $500,000 of active business income vs your marginal personal rate (up to 33% federal at high incomes). The main benefit is income deferral — leave retained earnings in the company, withdraw only what you need personally. Incorporation cost: $1,500–$3,000. Most break even within 12 months.

How accurate is this calculator?

Uses CRA 2026 federal brackets, all 10 provincial tax tables, CPP rates (11.9% on net income above $3,500 up to YMPE $73,200), Basic Personal Amounts (federal $16,129 + province-specific), and CRA mileage rates. Accurate for straightforward self-employment income. Complex situations (RRSP, capital gains, HST ITCs, detailed vehicle method) should be verified with a licensed CPA.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I pay double CPP as a gig worker?

Employed Canadians split CPP 50/50 with their employer. As a self-employed gig worker, you are your own employer — so you pay both halves. In 2026 that means 11.9% of net self-employment income (after the $3,500 basic exemption), up to maximum insurable earnings of $73,200. On $60,000 net income, that's approximately $7,100 in CPP alone.

What is the GST/HST $30,000 threshold exactly?

Once your total worldwide taxable supplies exceed $30,000 in any single calendar quarter, or over four consecutive quarters, you must register for GST/HST within 29 days. Failure = CRA will assess back-GST on your gross revenue (meaning you absorb the cost), plus a 6% penalty. Register proactively — you can claim Input Tax Credits immediately.

Can I deduct home office costs even as a gig worker?

Yes. CRA allows home office deductions for self-employed individuals if the space is used exclusively and regularly for business. Calculate the business-use percentage based on square footage. Deductible: rent/mortgage interest, utilities (proportional), internet, property taxes (proportional). Keep receipts for everything — CRA digital audits are increasingly data-driven.

Should I pay taxes in quarterly instalments?

If your net tax owing exceeds $3,000 ($1,800 in Quebec) for both the current AND either of the two previous years, CRA requires quarterly instalments: March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15. Missing instalments triggers interest at the prescribed rate (currently 9% annualized for 2026). Set up ennakkovero-style auto-transfers to avoid surprise bills.

Is it worth incorporating as a freelancer in Canada?

Generally yes if your net income consistently exceeds $80,000–$100,000/year. A Canadian Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC) pays 9% federal small business tax on the first $500,000 of active business income, vs your marginal personal rate (33%+ federally at high incomes). The key advantage: income deferral — you leave retained earnings in the corporation and withdraw only what you need personally.

How accurate is this calculator?

It provides accurate estimates for straightforward self-employment income.

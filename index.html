Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I pay double CPP as a gig worker?

Employed Canadians split CPP 50/50 with their employer. As a self-employed worker you pay both halves — currently 11.9% of net income above $3,500, up to the YMPE of $73,200. On $60,000 net income: ~$7,100 CPP vs ~$3,550 for a salaried employee at the same gross. This is the single largest hidden cost of gig work in Canada.

What is the GST/HST $30,000 threshold?

Once total taxable supplies exceed $30,000 in any single quarter or over four consecutive quarters, you must register within 29 days. Failing to register = CRA assesses the GST on past revenue (you absorb the cost) + 6% penalty + compounded daily interest. After registering you claim Input Tax Credits on all business purchases, partially offsetting the cost.

Can I deduct home office costs?

Yes, for self-employed individuals if the space is used exclusively and regularly for business. Calculate the percentage by square footage. Deductible: proportional rent or mortgage interest, utilities, internet, property taxes. Keep monthly receipts — CRA digital audits now match declared deductions against bank transaction patterns.

When do I need quarterly instalments?

If net tax owing exceeds $3,000 ($1,800 in Quebec) for both the current year and either of the two prior years, CRA requires instalments: March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15. Missing these triggers 9% annualized prescribed-rate interest in 2026.

Is incorporating (CCPC) worth it for gig workers?

Generally yes if net income consistently exceeds $80,000–$100,000. A CCPC pays 9% federal small business rate on first $500,000 of active income vs your personal marginal rate. Main benefit: income deferral — leave retained earnings in the company, withdraw only what you need personally. Incorporation: $1,500–$3,000. Most gig workers break even within 12 months.

How accurate is this calculator?

Uses CRA 2026 federal brackets, all 10 provincial tax tables, CPP rates (11.9% on net income above $3,500 up to YMPE $73,200), Basic Personal Amounts (federal $16,129 + province-specific), and CRA published mileage rates. Accurate for straightforward self-employment. Complex situations (RRSP, capital gains, HST ITCs) should be verified with a CPA.
